# Relationships — What counts as a win

A Relationships win is anything that nurtures connection — with friends, family,
a romantic partner, community, or yourself. Self-love counts here. Showing up for
someone counts. A text back counts. It doesn't need to be a deep conversation.

## Examples

**Friends**
- Reached out to a friend, even just to check in
- Made plans with someone
- Showed up for someone who needed it
- Had a real conversation instead of surface-level small talk
- Let a friend show up for you

**Family**
- Called or texted a family member
- Had a difficult but necessary family conversation
- Spent quality time with family
- Set a boundary with family in a way that protected both of you
- Forgave something

**Romantic partner / love**
- Had a real conversation with a partner
- Did something kind for your partner unprompted
- Expressed how you feel instead of keeping it in
- Set a healthy boundary in the relationship
- Noticed something good about the relationship and said it out loud
- Had a hard conversation and stayed present through it

**Community and belonging**
- Met someone new
- Showed up at an event or gathering
- Contributed to a group, team, or community
- Helped someone without being asked

**Self-relationship**
- Did something kind for yourself with no productivity justification
- Said no to something that wasn't good for you
- Honored your own feelings instead of dismissing them
- Treated yourself the way you would treat someone you care about
- Spent time alone and actually enjoyed it

**Repair and honesty**
- Apologized when you should have
- Said something true that was hard to say
- Let go of a grudge or resentment, even slightly
- Reached out after a gap and reconnected

## Translation examples

> "texted my mom back finally"
→ Maintained a family connection. Presence matters even in small doses.

> "had a really honest conversation with my partner about something we'd been avoiding"
→ Chose truth over comfort. That's how relationships actually grow.

> "went to the event even though I was tired"
→ Invested in community and showed up for connection when it would have been easy not to.

> "let myself be sad about the friendship ending instead of pretending it was fine"
→ Honored the relationship by grieving it honestly. That's love, not weakness.

> "booked a solo dinner because I wanted to"
→ Practiced enjoying your own company. Self-relationship is real.
