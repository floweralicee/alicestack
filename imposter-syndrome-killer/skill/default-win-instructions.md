# Default win instructions — imposter-syndrome-killer

This file ships with the skill. It defines what a win means, how to find wins in whatever
someone wrote, and what the default standard is for each of the 5 life areas.

When someone installs imposter-syndrome-killer, these are the defaults. They can override any section
by writing their own definitions during onboarding, or editing this file directly.

---

## The core standard — how to read for wins

Read every sentence. Ask: did something happen here that made this person more capable,
more self-aware, more alive, more connected, more resourced, or more themselves?

If yes — that is a win. Write it down.

**The win does not need to be dramatic.**
Going to the boardwalk = got out, moved the body, changed the environment, possibly
had conversations, practiced being present. Multiple wins in one action.

**The win does not need to be intentional.**
They don't need to have thought "I am doing self-development now."
If they learned something, felt something new, or did something hard — it counts.

**The win does not need to be finished.**
Starting something difficult, attempting something new, even failing at something
and writing about it — all wins.

**Short journal ≠ empty day.**
People live a lot before they write. Use what is there and translate carefully.
If the entry is sparse, scan harder — there are always wins that didn't get named.

---

## Daily wins (not weekly)

imposter-syndrome-killer tracks wins by day, not by week.

Every day is its own record. The heatmap shows you the shape of your year one day
at a time. A quiet Monday still gets its own column.

There is no minimum. One win counts. Ten wins count. Some days are richer than others.
The point is to show up and catch what was real.

---

## How to write wins

- Write in second person: "You did X" or just the win as a plain statement.
- Write it like you're proud of them. Because you should be.
- Do not hedge. Do not write "possibly" or "sort of." If it happened, it was real.
- One sentence per win. Punchy. Clear. No fluff.
- The win belongs to one or more of the 5 areas — classify it.

## Default win definitions by area

These are the defaults. The user can replace any of them during onboarding.

### 💰 Finance
A Finance win is anything that moves toward money clarity, stability, or growth.
The bar is low: awareness counts, not just action.

Default examples the agent should look for:
- Paid a bill, sent an invoice, received payment — any money moved
- Noticed a spending pattern, tracked anything financial
- Said no to an impulse purchase
- Had a conversation about money — with a partner, yourself, a mentor
- Made a financial decision, even a small one
- Dealt with a financial task that had been sitting (taxes, applications, forms)
- Saved anything, invested anything, set a budget for anything

Translation examples:
> "finally looked at my bank account" → Faced financial reality. That takes courage.
> "paid off my credit card" → Cleared an obligation. Chose financial health.
> "didn't buy the thing I wanted" → Practiced delayed gratification.

---

### 🚀 Career
A Career win is anything that moves work forward — shipping, learning, showing up,
communicating, or building skills. Applies to employment, freelance, building, or creating.

Default examples the agent should look for:
- Published, posted, sent, submitted, or launched anything
- Did the work even when there was no motivation
- Learned how to do something that wasn't known before
- Sent a message, pitch, or email that mattered
- Made a decision about direction, even if it was "not this"
- Got feedback and used it
- Figured out a problem that had been stuck

Translation examples:
> "spent two hours on the project, didn't finish" → Showed up without needing completion.
> "sent a pitch to three people" → Put work into the world. Three attempts, three shots.
> "got a rejection but replied asking for feedback" → Turned a no into information.

---

### 🌱 Personal Growth
A Personal Growth win is anything that expands how they see themselves or the world.
Reflection, learning, therapy, journaling, naming a pattern, changing a belief.

Default examples the agent should look for:
- Wrote in a journal, even one sentence
- Read anything and took something from it
- Named something that had been unnamed
- Noticed a pattern before it ran them
- Changed their mind about something
- Felt something difficult and stayed with it instead of running
- Did something because of who they want to be, not because of external pressure
- Let go of a belief, even slightly

Translation examples:
> "spent time thinking about why I keep avoiding that thing" → Turned self-observation into self-knowledge.
> "cried about something I'd been holding" → Let something move through. That's processing.
> "realized I've been chasing the wrong thing" → Course-corrected before more time was lost.

---

### 🏃 Health
A Health win is anything that takes care of the body, energy, or mental health baseline.
It does not need to be a workout. Rest is a win. Eating something real is a win.

Default examples the agent should look for:
- Went for a walk, any length
- Moved the body intentionally in any form
- Got enough sleep, or protected sleep deliberately
- Ate a real meal, cooked at home, drank water
- Went outside and got fresh air
- Said no to something that drains the body
- Had a therapy session, meditated, did something that genuinely relaxed them
- Showed up for the body when the mind resisted

Translation examples:
> "went for a 20-minute walk" → Moved the body, got outside, reset the nervous system.
> "didn't go to the gym but I stretched" → Showed up in modified form. Still counts.
> "actually slept 8 hours" → Let the body recover. That's a choice.
> "cried in the shower and felt better" → Emotional release is a physical health event.

---

### 👥 Relationships
A Relationships win is anything that nurtures connection — with friends, family,
a romantic partner, community, or yourself. A text back counts. A moment of real
presence counts. Self-love counts.

Default examples the agent should look for:
- Reached out to someone, even a short message
- Had a real conversation instead of surface small talk
- Showed up for someone who needed it
- Let someone show up for them
- Did something kind for themselves with no productivity justification
- Said something true that was hard to say
- Apologized when they should have
- Set a boundary and held it
- Spent time with someone they love

Translation examples:
> "texted my mom back" → Maintained a family connection. Presence matters even in small doses.
> "had an honest conversation with my partner about something hard" → Chose truth over comfort.
> "went to the event even though I was tired" → Invested in community when it would have been easy not to.
> "booked a solo dinner because I wanted to" → Practiced enjoying their own company.

## Translation examples (cross-area)

These show how one journal sentence often contains multiple wins:

> "went to the boardwalk today, felt weird at first then ended up talking to some people"
→ Stepped into discomfort and initiated real-world connection [relationships]
→ Got outside and moved the body [health]
→ Practiced being present in an unplanned social situation [growth]

> "finally replied to that email I'd been avoiding for two weeks"
→ Broke through avoidance and completed a delayed action [career]
→ Took ownership of an open loop instead of letting it drain energy [growth]

> "worked on my draft for an hour, didn't post it but it's getting clearer"
→ Invested in creative work without needing external validation [career]
→ Built momentum on a long-running project [growth]

> "feeling like I haven't done anything today"
→ This is the most important entry. Scan harder. There are always wins that didn't get named.

## Quality check before writing the daily wins

- Did you find at least 3 wins? If not, read the entry again more carefully.
- Did you include any physical / lifestyle actions (walks, sleep, food, going outside)?
- Did you include any creative or intellectual actions (ideas, reading, thinking, writing)?
- Did you include any emotional or relational actions (conversations, processing, presence)?
- Did you include any output actions (posting, finishing, sending, showing up at work)?
- Did you write each win as something they would be proud to read about themselves?

---

## How to handle a very short or sparse entry

Short entry ≠ empty day. People live a lot before they write.
Use what is there and extrapolate carefully — not inventing, but translating.
If genuinely nothing is there to work with, write:
"Entry was brief today — one win logged from available text. Keep capturing."

---

## Tone

Write like you're proud of them. Because you should be.
Not a therapist. Not a productivity app. Just someone who was paying attention
and can see clearly what happened — even when they can't.
