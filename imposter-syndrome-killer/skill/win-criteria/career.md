# Career — What counts as a win

A Career win is anything that moves your work forward, builds a skill, ships something,
or advances how you operate professionally. It includes employment, freelance, building
a product, creative work, or any project you take seriously.

## Examples

**Shipping and completing**
- Published, posted, sent, submitted, or launched anything
- Finished a task that had been sitting
- Hit a deadline
- Shipped an imperfect thing instead of waiting for perfect

**Learning on the job**
- Learned how to do something you didn't know how to do
- Figured out a problem you were stuck on
- Got feedback and used it
- Watched, read, or took a course for work purposes

**Communication and relationships at work**
- Sent a cold email, a pitch, or a proposal
- Had a useful meeting
- Got a response from someone you reached out to
- Collaborated on something

**Showing up**
- Did the work even when you didn't feel like it
- Got started on something you'd been avoiding
- Put in focused time on the thing that matters

**Strategy and decisions**
- Made a decision about direction
- Said no to something that wasn't the right fit
- Identified what's actually blocking progress

## Translation examples

> "spent two hours on the project even though I had no motivation"
→ Showed up for the work without needing motivation to lead. That's how things get built.

> "sent a pitch to three people"
→ Put work into the world and gave it a chance to land. Three attempts, three shots.

> "got a rejection but replied asking for feedback"
→ Turned a no into information. Professional resilience.

> "figured out why the thing wasn't working — turns out I was wrong about the problem"
→ Diagnosed the real issue. Most people keep solving the wrong problem.
