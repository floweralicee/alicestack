# Personal Growth — What counts as a win

A Personal Growth win is anything that expands how you see yourself or the world.
It includes learning, reflection, therapy, journaling, changing a belief, naming a pattern,
facing something uncomfortable, or becoming slightly more yourself.

## Examples

**Learning and reading**
- Read anything — a book, an article, a thread — and absorbed something from it
- Watched something educational or thought-provoking
- Listened to a podcast and had an idea
- Took any kind of class or lesson

**Reflection and journaling**
- Wrote in a journal
- Thought through a problem and reached a new perspective
- Named something that had been unnamed
- Noticed your own pattern before it ran you

**Mindset and identity shifts**
- Changed your mind about something
- Let go of a belief that was holding you back
- Stopped comparing yourself to someone else for a moment
- Recognized that something you've been doing is avoidance

**Emotional growth**
- Felt something difficult and stayed with it instead of running
- Named a fear clearly
- Processed something that had been sitting unprocessed
- Showed yourself compassion when you would usually be hard on yourself

**Action from insight**
- Did something because you decided who you want to be, not because of external pressure
- Took ownership of something instead of blaming the situation
- Made a decision based on your own values, not someone else's expectations

## Translation examples

> "spent some time thinking about why I keep avoiding that one thing"
→ Turned self-observation into self-knowledge. Most people never look at their own patterns.

> "cried about something I'd been holding for a long time"
→ Let something move through instead of staying stuck. Emotional processing is growth.

> "realized I've been chasing the wrong thing for a few months"
→ Course-corrected before more time was lost. Clarity is a win even when it's uncomfortable.

> "wrote three pages, wasn't sure why, just needed to"
→ Used writing as thinking. That's exactly what it's for.
