# Health — What counts as a win

A Health win is anything that takes care of your body, energy, or mental health baseline.
It does not need to be a workout. Rest is a win. Eating something decent is a win.
The bar is: did you do something that was better for your body than not doing it?

## Examples

**Movement**
- Went for a walk — any length
- Worked out, exercised, stretched, did yoga
- Moved your body intentionally in any way
- Took the stairs, walked somewhere instead of driving

**Sleep**
- Got enough sleep, or protected your sleep deliberately
- Went to bed earlier than usual
- Put the phone down before sleeping
- Let yourself rest when your body asked for it

**Food and hydration**
- Ate a real meal instead of skipping
- Cooked at home
- Drank water consistently
- Made a healthier choice than usual, even once

**Mental health**
- Had a session with a therapist or counselor
- Meditated, even for 5 minutes
- Did something that genuinely relaxed you
- Took a break when you needed one instead of pushing through
- Went outside and got fresh air

**Saying no to bad things**
- Skipped alcohol, junk food, or something that drains you
- Set a limit on screen time
- Protected energy by declining something draining

**Showing up for your body when you didn't want to**
- Went to the gym even though you really didn't want to
- Made the doctor/dentist appointment
- Did the thing your body needed even when your mind resisted

## Translation examples

> "went for a 20-minute walk in the afternoon"
→ Moved the body, got outside, reset the nervous system.

> "didn't go to the gym but I did stretch and walk around the block"
→ Maintained physical care in a modified form. Showed up anyway.

> "actually slept 8 hours for once"
→ Let the body recover. That's a choice that takes discipline.

> "cooked dinner instead of ordering again"
→ Took care of the body with real food and resisted the easy option.

> "cried in the shower and then felt better"
→ Emotional release is a physical health event. The body needed that.
