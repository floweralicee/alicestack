# Finance — What counts as a win

A Finance win is anything that moves you toward financial awareness, stability, or growth.
It does not need to involve large amounts of money. Small acts of financial responsibility count.

## Examples

**Earning**
- Made any money — a paycheck, a payment received, a sale, a tip, a refund
- Got a raise, a bonus, or a new client
- Shipped something that could eventually make money

**Spending awareness**
- Tracked spending, even for one category
- Noticed a pattern in where money goes
- Said no to an impulse purchase
- Chose the cheaper option consciously

**Bills and obligations**
- Paid a bill on time
- Set up auto-pay for something
- Dealt with a financial task you'd been avoiding (taxes, invoices, applications)

**Saving and growing**
- Put any amount into savings
- Invested, even a small amount
- Opened an account, set a budget, made a financial plan

**Financial conversations**
- Had a real conversation about money — with a partner, a mentor, yourself
- Researched something financial you didn't understand
- Negotiated a price, a rate, or a deal

**Financial mindset**
- Noticed anxiety about money and named it instead of avoiding it
- Changed a belief about what you deserve to earn
- Stopped equating your worth with your income

## What does NOT count
- Just thinking about money without any action or awareness shift
- Receiving money passively with no reflection (rent auto-deposited, etc.)

## Translation examples

> "paid off my credit card this month"
→ Cleared a financial obligation and reduced debt. Chose financial health over spending.

> "finally sent that invoice I'd been putting off for two weeks"
→ Broke financial avoidance and claimed money owed.

> "didn't buy the thing I wanted even though I really wanted it"
→ Practiced delayed gratification. Financial self-discipline.

> "looked at my bank account for the first time in a while"
→ Faced financial reality. That takes courage for a lot of people.
